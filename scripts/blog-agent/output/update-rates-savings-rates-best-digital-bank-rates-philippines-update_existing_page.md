## What to Look at Beyond the Headline Rate

A high advertised rate is only part of the picture. Before choosing
a digital bank based on interest rate alone, check these factors:

### 1. Is the rate conditional or unconditional?
Some banks (like Maya Bank) require monthly spending missions or
activity conditions to unlock their highest rate. If you cannot
consistently meet those conditions, your effective rate will be
lower than the headline figure.

### 2. Does a balance cap apply?
Many digital banks apply their highest rate only up to a maximum
balance (e.g., ₱100,000 or ₱50,000). Amounts above the cap earn
a lower rate. For larger savings, this significantly affects your
total interest earned.

### 3. Is the account PDIC-insured?
All BSP-licensed digital banks in the Philippines are covered by
the Philippine Deposit Insurance Corporation (PDIC) up to
**₱500,000 per depositor per bank**. This is a critical safety
feature. Do not deposit with any entity that is not PDIC-insured.

### 4. What is the minimum maintaining balance?
Most Philippine digital banks require zero or very low maintaining
balances — one of their key advantages over traditional banks.
Always confirm this before opening an account.

### 5. Time deposit vs. savings account
Time deposits offer higher rates but lock your money in for a
fixed period. Regular savings accounts are more liquid. If you
may need access to your funds, a locked time deposit is not
suitable for that portion of your money.

## Are Digital Banks in the Philippines Safe?

Yes — BSP-licensed digital banks in the Philippines operate under
the same regulatory framework as traditional banks and are subject
to Bangko Sentral ng Pilipinas (BSP) oversight. All BSP-licensed
digital banks are members of PDIC, which insures deposits up to
₱500,000 per depositor per bank.

**To verify a digital bank's BSP license and PDIC membership:**
- Check the BSP website: [www.bsp.gov.ph](https://www.bsp.gov.ph)
- Check the PDIC website: [www.pdic.gov.ph](https://www.pdic.gov.ph)

Do not deposit money with any savings app or platform that is not
on the BSP's list of licensed banks or PDIC's list of member
institutions.
